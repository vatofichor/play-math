Why Pi remains constant

A circle is perfectly symmetrical. If you scale its diameter by any factor, its circumference scales by that exact same factor. Because they grow in perfect proportion, their ratio $\frac{C}{d}$ is a universal constant. We designate this constant by the Greek letter $\mathbf{\pi}$ (Pi).
