What are Units?

Before standard rulers existed, people used body parts like hand spans, footsteps, or paperclips. Try measuring the board with different items below!

Length = 10 Child Hands

Why Standard Units Matter

Notice that child hands are smaller, so it takes more of them to cover the board. Adult hands are larger, so it takes fewer. This difference causes confusion!

Standard units (like centimeters, meters, inches) are identical everywhere, ensuring measurements are the same no matter who takes them!

Standard Units Fact Sheet

• Non-Standard: Hand spans, paperclips, footsteps. They vary in size.
• Standard: Centimeters, Inches, Grams, Liters. They are exactly identical everywhere.
• Rule: The smaller the unit of measurement, the larger the number of units you will count for the same object!
