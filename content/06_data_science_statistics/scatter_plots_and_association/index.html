Concept Leverage vs. Influence

Leverage refers to how extreme a data point's predictor value ($x$) is relative to the mean of $x$.

An outlier with high leverage can act as a pivot point, exerting massive influence that pulls the entire regression line towards it and artificially inflating or deflating correlation index ($r$).

Click "Load Preset Outlier" to observe a classic high-influence leverage point and see how removing it restores a perfect $r = 1.0$ fit!
