MAD Reference Rules


  • Deviations: The distance of any value $x$ from the mean $\bar{x}$ is calculated as absolute value: $|x - \bar{x}|$. Stems are always positive lengths.
  • Definition: MAD is the arithmetic average of these individual distances. It describes the typical distance a data point lies from the center.
  • Variability: A smaller MAD indicates that the points are clustered close to the mean (high consistency). A larger MAD indicates spread (high variability).
