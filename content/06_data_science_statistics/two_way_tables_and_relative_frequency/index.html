Two-Way Table Definitions


  • Joint Frequency: The entries inside the cells of the table that represent counts of subjects matching both row and column categories (e.g. 7th Graders who like Math).
  • Marginal Frequency: The totals along the bottom row or rightmost column representing totals of single categories.
  • Joint Relative Frequency: Cell count divided by grand total.
  • Conditional Relative Frequency: Cell count divided by its row total (or column total). Critical for verifying dependency.
  • Checking Association: If the conditional relative frequencies of column options differ significantly between rows, the variables are associated.
