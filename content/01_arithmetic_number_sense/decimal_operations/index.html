Rule of Decimal Alignment

When adding or subtracting, alignment by place value is critical. Unlike integers where alignment is right-justified, decimals must align precisely around the decimal point.

Unlined decimal operations yield garbage values (e.g. adding 0.5 to 0.05 is NOT 0.10, it is 0.55!).
