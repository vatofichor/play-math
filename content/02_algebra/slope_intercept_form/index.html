Physics of Steepness

Ramp formula: y = mx + b.
• A steeper slope (larger $|m|$) applies more directional gravity force, resulting in faster acceleration.
• Y-intercept ($b$) represents the starting height. Increasing $b$ raises the drop point, resulting in a longer ride and higher top speed.

Linear Equations Guide

• **Linear Equation:** $y = mx + b$
• **Slope ($m$):** $\frac{\text{Change in } y}{\text{Change in } x} = \frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}$
• **Positive Slope:** Rises left-to-right
• **Negative Slope:** Falls left-to-right
• **Zero Slope:** Flat horizontal line ($y = b$)
• **Y-Intercept ($b$):** Crossing point $(0, b)$
