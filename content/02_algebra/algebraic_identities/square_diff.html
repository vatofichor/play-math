Proof

Algebraic Expansion

Let's prove this algebraic identity by distributing the negative term:

1. Expand the exponent as a binomial product:
(a - b)² = (a - b)(a - b)

2. Distribute terms using the distributive law:
= a(a - b) - b(a - b)

3. Distribute variables into the terms (be careful of signs!):
= a² - ab - ba + b²

4. Group the two matching negative terms (ab = ba):
= a² - 2ab + b²
